Reference Bibliography

Bibliography

Core references for the theory and methods in these notes

The following references are organised by topic. Entries marked [Essential] are the most important for following the course; those marked [Further reading] go deeper into specific areas.

Model Selection

Akaike, H. (1974)
A new look at the statistical model identification. IEEE Transactions on Automatic Control, 19(6), 716–723. AIC origin paper [Essential]
The foundational paper introducing AIC. Akaike derives the criterion from the perspective of minimising the expected Kullback–Leibler divergence between the fitted model and the truth. A landmark in the history of statistics.
Schwarz, G. (1978)
Estimating the dimension of a model. The Annals of Statistics, 6(2), 461–464. BIC origin paper [Essential]
The original two-page derivation of BIC via Laplace approximation to the marginal likelihood. Concise and elegant — worth reading in full.
Hurvich, C. M. and Tsai, C.-L. (1989)
Regression and time series model selection in small samples. Biometrika, 76(2), 297–307. AICc
Derives the small-sample corrected AIC (AIC$_c$) for normal linear regression.
Takeuchi, K. (1976)
Distribution of informational statistics and a criterion of model fitting. Suri-Kagaku (Mathematical Sciences), 153, 12–18. (In Japanese.) TIC
Introduces the TIC, which generalises AIC to the misspecified case by using the sandwich correction $\mathrm{tr}(J^{-1}K)$ instead of $p$.
Burnham, K. P. and Anderson, D. R. (2002)
Model Selection and Multimodel Inference: A Practical Information-Theoretic Approach (2nd edn.). Springer, New York. AIC in ecology [Further reading]
The definitive applied guide to AIC-based model selection. Heavy on practical guidance and ecological applications.
Claeskens, G. and Hjort, N. L. (2008)
Model Selection and Model Averaging. Cambridge University Press. Theory [Further reading]
A rigorous mathematical treatment of focused information criteria (FIC) and model averaging. Excellent for understanding the asymptotic theory deeply.
Stone, M. (1977)
An asymptotic equivalence of choice of model by cross-validation and Akaike's criterion. Journal of the Royal Statistical Society B, 39(1), 44–47. AIC = LOO-CV
Proves that AIC and leave-one-out cross-validation are asymptotically equivalent for linear models — a beautiful connection.
Kass, R. E. and Raftery, A. E. (1995)
Bayes factors. Journal of the American Statistical Association, 90(430), 773–795. Bayes factors [Essential]
The canonical reference on Bayes factors: definition, computation, interpretation scale, and relationship to BIC. Highly readable.
Hoeting, J. A., Madigan, D., Raftery, A. E. and Volinsky, C. T. (1999)
Bayesian model averaging: a tutorial. Statistical Science, 14(4), 382–401. Model averaging [Further reading]

Generalised Linear Models

McCullagh, P. and Nelder, J. A. (1989)
Generalised Linear Models (2nd edn.). Chapman and Hall, London. GLMs [Essential]
The definitive textbook on GLMs. Comprehensive treatment of exponential families, link functions, IWLS, residuals, and model checking. A must-read for anyone working seriously with regression models.
Wedderburn, R. W. M. (1974)
Quasi-likelihood functions, generalized linear models, and the Gauss–Newton method. Biometrika, 61(3), 439–447. Quasi-likelihood [Essential]
Introduces the quasi-likelihood framework, showing that valid inference only requires a correct mean–variance specification, not a full distributional assumption.
Liang, K.-Y. and Zeger, S. L. (1986)
Longitudinal data analysis using generalized linear models. Biometrika, 73(1), 13–22. GEE [Essential]
The original GEE paper. Proposes estimating equations for clustered/longitudinal data with a working correlation matrix and sandwich variance estimator.

Mixed Models

Laird, N. M. and Ware, J. H. (1982)
Random-effects models for longitudinal data. Biometrics, 38(4), 963–974. LMM [Essential]
The landmark paper establishing the EM algorithm for fitting linear mixed models. Defines the model, discusses REML, and gives the BLUP equations.
Pinheiro, J. C. and Bates, D. M. (2000)
Mixed-Effects Models in S and S-PLUS. Springer, New York. LMM / NLME / R [Essential]
The definitive practical guide to fitting mixed models in R using the nlme package. Covers linear and nonlinear mixed models in depth, with extensive real data examples.
Bates, D., Mächler, M., Bolker, B. and Walker, S. (2015)
Fitting linear mixed-effects models using lme4. Journal of Statistical Software, 67(1), 1–48. lme4 / R [Essential]
The paper describing the lme4 package. Explains the sparse matrix methods, profiled likelihood, and REML implementation used by lmer().
Goldstein, H. (2011)
Multilevel Statistical Models (4th edn.). Wiley, Chichester. Multilevel models [Further reading]
Breslow, N. E. and Clayton, D. G. (1993)
Approximate inference in generalized linear mixed models. Journal of the American Statistical Association, 88(421), 9–25. GLMM / PQL / Laplace [Essential]
Introduces PQL and the Laplace approximation for GLMMs. Also shows that PQL can be biased for binary responses with small cluster sizes.
Liu, Q. and Pierce, D. A. (1994)
A note on Gauss–Hermite quadrature. Biometrika, 81(3), 624–629. Quadrature for GLMMs

Nonlinear Models

Seber, G. A. F. and Wild, C. J. (2003)
Nonlinear Regression. Wiley-Interscience, Hoboken, NJ. NLS theory [Essential]
Comprehensive treatment of nonlinear least squares — estimation, inference, diagnostics, profile likelihood intervals, and many worked examples.
Lindstrom, M. J. and Bates, D. M. (1990)
Nonlinear mixed effects models for repeated measures data. Biometrics, 46(3), 673–687. NLME [Essential]
Proposes and analyses the nonlinear mixed effects model using Laplace approximation for the marginal likelihood.
Davidian, M. and Giltinan, D. M. (1995)
Nonlinear Models for Repeated Measurement Data. Chapman and Hall, London. NLME applications [Further reading]
Focuses on pharmacokinetic and biological applications of nonlinear mixed models. Excellent for practitioners.

Latent Variables and the EM Algorithm

Dempster, A. P., Laird, N. M. and Rubin, D. B. (1977)
Maximum likelihood from incomplete data via the EM algorithm. Journal of the Royal Statistical Society B, 39(1), 1–38. EM algorithm [Essential]
The original EM paper — one of the most cited papers in the history of statistics. Introduces the E and M steps, proves monotone ascent, discusses convergence, and applies EM to a wide range of problems.
McLachlan, G. J. and Krishnan, T. (2007)
The EM Algorithm and Extensions (2nd edn.). Wiley, New York. EM — comprehensive [Essential]
The most complete treatment of EM available. Covers convergence theory, extensions (ECM, ECME, MCEM), and hundreds of applications.
McLachlan, G. J. and Peel, D. (2000)
Finite Mixture Models. Wiley, New York. Mixture models [Essential]
Definitive reference on mixture models — identifiability, EM, model selection for $K$, extensions to mixtures of regressions and hidden Markov models.
Fraley, C. and Raftery, A. E. (2002)
Model-based clustering, discriminant analysis, and density estimation. Journal of the American Statistical Association, 97(458), 611–631. mclust / BIC for K [Essential]
Describes the mclust R package methodology: model-based clustering with a rich family of Gaussian mixture covariance structures, using BIC for model selection.
Tanner, M. A. and Wong, W. H. (1987)
The calculation of posterior distributions by data augmentation. Journal of the American Statistical Association, 82(398), 528–540. Data augmentation [Further reading]
Introduces data augmentation as a general technique for Bayesian computation with latent variables, foreshadowing the MCMC revolution.
Roeder, K. (1990)
Density estimation with confidence sets exemplified by superclusters and voids in the galaxies. Journal of the American Statistical Association, 85(411), 617–624. Galaxy data
Source of the famous galaxies dataset used throughout Chapter 4.

Bayesian Inference

Gelman, A., Carlin, J. B., Stern, H. S., Dunson, D. B., Vehtari, A. and Rubin, D. B. (2013)
Bayesian Data Analysis (3rd edn.). CRC Press, Boca Raton. Bayesian [Essential]
The standard graduate-level Bayesian textbook. Covers hierarchical models, MCMC, model checking, and much more. Freely available online.
Berger, J. O. (1985)
Statistical Decision Theory and Bayesian Analysis (2nd edn.). Springer, New York. Decision theory / Bayes [Further reading]

Computation and Software

R Core Team (2024)
R: A Language and Environment for Statistical Computing. R Foundation for Statistical Computing, Vienna, Austria. https://www.R-project.org/
Venables, W. N. and Ripley, B. D. (2002)
Modern Applied Statistics with S (4th edn.). Springer, New York. MASS / R [Essential]
Companion to the MASS R package. Covers GLMs, robust methods, survival analysis, multivariate methods, and more.
